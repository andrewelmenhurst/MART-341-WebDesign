Marathon Training Instructable

Step 1 - Tie Your Shoes Correctly

Tie your shoes in the instructed fashion to support your arches and ankles.

Step 2 - Perfect your running form before running for time.

This is the most critical step that will enable your body to move efficiently. Keep your shoulders relaxed, jaw loose, and run with your hands moving forward to back without swaying to the sides.

Step 3 - Create a block schedule of runs that works with your life schedule.

Contrary to popular belief, your marathon training schedule should not overtake your life. Find time in your week to implement at least one short run, one long run, and one medium run. As you progress through training, add speed work and body weight exercises. A full training schedule can be around ten hours a week.
