The Perks of Being a Wallflower

Stephen Chbosky

A socially awkard teen named Charlie begins highschool as with little confidence and little hope of fitting in. However, his first day of class he catches notice of a funny, charismatic individual by the name of Patrick.

Charlie hesistates and rescinds the idea approaching Patrick outside of class that day. Soon after, Charlie attends the highschool's first football game, a team that his brother stuck out as skilled quarterback. Patrick notices Charlie and calls him over. With Patrick sits his step-sister, Sam. Charlie instantly falls in love. The three highschoolers continue to frolic into the night at a party where Charlie feels a sense of being watching this beautiful reality unfold around him.

As Fall turns into Winter, the friendship of these three grows stronger, and Charlie begins to witness the love, laughter, trauma, and pain of loving someone so strongly as he does with Sam.

Charlie never gets the chance to profess his love to Sam, as she dated another man with a taste for infidelity. Furthermore, Charlie begins to date another girl in the friend group for which he has no genuine interest.

The unrequitted love of Charlie leads to a tension filled night of games where Charlie is asked to kiss the most beautiful woman in the room. He kisses Sam thus evicerating dynamic of the friend group.

Ultimately, Charlie digresses into a manic episode of self-hatred and past trauma. He doesn't see his friends for some time, and wallows in lonliness. Until the bitter end of the year, where Charlie's friends will graduate, he speaks to Sam. They engage in a long, affectionate talk about feeling so strongly for another, and how scared Charlie is to endure the rest of highschool without Sam and Pat. Sam gives Charlie his first kiss, and he begins to understand that they'll never really be removed from eachother. His friends will be his friends whether they're on the east coast of up in Seattle. This reassures his ability to persist against the bitter existence of being in highschool.
